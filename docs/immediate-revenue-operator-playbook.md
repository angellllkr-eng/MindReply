# MindReply Immediate Revenue Operator Playbook

**Target:** 10+ paid Message Rescue purchases per day.

**Primary paid offer:** Message Rescue, GBP 49. Buyer pays once, pastes up to 3 hard messages, and gets send-ready wording inside the Message Rescue workspace.

**Operating rule:** Every action today must push one of three outcomes: reply YES, open checkout, or book a same-day rescue call. Anything else is delay.

---

## 1. Real Buyer

**Exact persona:** Founder-owner or senior biller at a boutique recruitment agency with 3-25 recruiters, selling contingency or retained hires into technology, finance, legal, healthcare, or executive-search roles.

**Environment:** They live in LinkedIn Recruiter, email, WhatsApp, ATS notes, and client calls. They are switching between candidates, hiring managers, fee objections, interview feedback, offer negotiation, rejection wording, and client follow-ups.

**Pain trigger:** They have 5-15 messages they know they should send but keep delaying because the wording affects money, reputation, or candidate trust.

**What they currently hate doing:** Rewriting sensitive client and candidate messages from scratch while already overloaded:

- Fee pushback replies
- Candidate rejection wording
- Hiring manager follow-ups
- Interview feedback chase messages
- Offer negotiation messages
- Apology messages after delays
- Boundary messages when clients demand free extra work

**What they lose weekly:** One delayed message can stall or kill a placement. A single contingency placement can be worth GBP 4,000-20,000. If they delay 10 important replies per week, the real loss is not writing time; it is stalled pipeline, candidate drop-off, client doubt, and lower close speed.

**Urgent buying moment:** They buy when a client, candidate, or hiring manager reply must go out today and they are stuck rewriting it for the third time.

## 5. Daily Pipeline Engine

**Daily minimum volume:** 350 direct outreaches per day.

**Split:**

- 250 LinkedIn DMs to recruitment founders, directors, senior billers
- 75 cold emails to agency owners and recruitment partners
- 25 warm/community replies in recruitment founder communities and LinkedIn comment threads

**Math required for 10 sales/day:**

- 350 outbound
- 18% reply rate = 63 replies
- 45% qualified pain replies = 28 buyers with a stuck message
- 36% close rate from qualified pain replies = 10 purchases

**Messaging adjustment if math misses:**

- If reply rate under 18%, make first line more painful and specific:
  > Fee pushback and candidate rejection messages are where recruitment owners leak time.
- If qualified pain under 45%, ask a sharper filter:
  > Do you have one reply today that you know should already be sent?
- If close rate under 36%, remove demo language and push checkout:
  > Use the GBP 49 rescue slot now. Paste 3 messages after checkout.

**Do not increase volume until message quality is fixed.**

## 7. Objection Handling

**Objection 1: "I can write it myself."**

> Correct. But you have not sent it yet. GBP 49 is not for writing ability; it is for removing the delay and getting the desk moving today.

**Objection 2: "GBP 49 is expensive for messages."**

> It is expensive only if the messages do not matter. If one delayed reply slows a placement, GBP 49 is the cheapest part of the day.

**Objection 3: "I do not trust AI with client messages."**

> Do not send it blindly. Use it as the final strong draft, then make your own judgment. The value is getting unstuck fast, not surrendering control.

**Objection 4: "I am busy."**

> That is exactly when this works. Paste the stuck message, take the send-ready version, move on. No onboarding.

**Objection 5: "Maybe later."**

> Later means the same reply sits in your head again tomorrow. If the message matters today, use the GBP 49 slot now. If it does not matter, ignore this.
